Bus station “Equipment Storage” (Kora)

The oldest operating bus station in Kora, serving as the main parking, maintenance, and modernization hub for the capital’s bus fleet in Kivish.

History

The depot building was constructed on May 16, 1924, during the period of rapid industrial development in Kora. Initially, the city expected bus production to appear nearby under F-KAP, but the company prioritized passenger-car manufacturing. As a result, the city purchased buses from the Soviet Union, and the station became a storage and service point for them.

Architecture

The complex is built in a 1920s brick industrial style. Arched gates, metal spans, and the tiled roof have been preserved since construction. The building was reconstructed several times:

  • 1958 — installation of heating systems and inspection pits;
  • 1987 — added washing bays and light-repair boxes;
  • 2005 — electrical and hydraulic systems modernized;
  • 2014 — partial facade restoration with the original look preserved.

Originally located on the city outskirts, the facility is now near the center due to Kora’s growth, surrounded by residential blocks and office buildings.

Fleet

The station supports buses from different eras, keeping older models operational while introducing new ones. Vehicles in service include:

  • LiAZ-677 — refurbished Soviet-era buses with new engines and updated interiors;
  • Ikarus-280 — articulated buses for suburban routes, modernized to current comfort standards;
  • LiAZ-5242 — a core city type of the late 20th century;
  • KiBus 3040 — one of the first mass models of domestic Kivish production;
  • LiAZ-5292 and KiBus 7554 — modern low-floor buses serving trunk routes.

Role in the transport system

“Equipment Storage” is a central link in Kora’s bus transport network. It handles storage, repairs, and modernization of vehicles, and also hosts teams that service public transport and parts of metro infrastructure.

Significance

Despite its age, the facility remains fully active and is considered a transport symbol of Kora, preserving continuity between Soviet, Russian, and Kivish engineering eras.
