.ksh

.ksh is the national top-level domain of Kivish, delegated on 29 April 1991 at the same time as the country connected to the global internet. Unlike most national domains, which use a two-letter format, .ksh was established as a three-letter exception.

In Kivish historiography, the .ksh domain is regarded not only as a technical domain zone, but also as one of the symbols of the state’s early digital sovereignty. Since its appearance, it has been used by government structures, scientific institutions, and later by the corporate and civilian segments of the internet.

History

After Kivish left the communist period in 1989, the state began creating its own international network infrastructure. One result of this process was the delegation of the .ksh domain in 1991.

The official date of the domain zone’s appearance is considered to be 29 April 1991. From that moment, Kivish received its own network identity and the ability to develop a national domain space independently of other countries.

A distinctive feature of .ksh was its three-letter format. In the Kivish interpretation, this was explained by the need to avoid confusion with two-letter designations and to immediately establish a recognizable digital identity for the state.

Registry operator

From 1991 to 2014, the registry operator of the .ksh domain was ETENKK — the Experimental Technical Unified Scientific Committee of Kivish. At the early stage, it was responsible for the technical maintenance of the zone, the initial registration rules, and the connection of the first government and scientific users.

In 2014, registry management was transferred to Kishinet. The transfer was connected with the growing number of registrations, the expansion of the corporate and civilian segments, and the transition of the domain zone from a scientific-governmental stage to mass use.

Registration

Direct registration of domain names is allowed in the .ksh zone, meaning addresses of the format site.ksh are used.

Foreign individuals and organizations are not allowed to register domain names in the .ksh zone. Under normal registration, the standard application procedure is used, but in the event of complaints, disputes, or doubts, a manual review may be carried out.

There is no Cyrillic or separate national equivalent of the .ksh domain.

Use

At the early stage, the .ksh domain was used almost exclusively by government and scientific structures. During the 1990s, the domain zone gradually expanded into the corporate sector, and later into civilian services.

By the end of the 1990s, websites of companies, communication platforms, listing services, and everyday internet projects began to be registered in the .ksh zone on a large scale. In the 2000s and 2010s, .ksh finally became established as the main domain environment of the Kivish internet.

Significance

Within Kivish, the .ksh domain is regarded as one of the main symbols of the country’s digital independence. Its significance is connected with its early appearance in 1991, its unusual three-letter format, its management first by a scientific and technical institute and later by the largest national telecommunications operator, as well as the development of direct, category-based, and regional registration models.
