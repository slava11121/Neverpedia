Hanul

Hanul (Korean: 하늘) is a state-owned automobile company of the Kivish Korean Peninsula, founded on 26 June 2026. The company was created as a new domestic automaker for the region and as an attempt to form its own automotive brand for the northern part of the Korean Peninsula against the background of the weak presence of South Korean brands.

Hanul’s first direction was the development of a mass-market passenger car for the domestic market. From the very beginning, the company began cooperation with Tarifa Auto, which had experience working in the KKP market, as well as with Japanese automotive representatives connected with Toyota. At the same time, engine production for future cars was planned to be developed inside Hanul itself.

History

Background

By the mid-2020s, the automobile market of the Kivish Korean Peninsula remained heavily dependent on external and allied manufacturers. South Korean cars, primarily Hyundai, were rare and failed to establish a stable position in the region. Against this background, the KKP leadership increasingly discussed the idea of creating its own automobile company that could work specifically for local conditions, roads, buyer habits, and the political and economic specifics of the autonomy.

A separate factor was the role of Tarifa Auto, whose models had already taken an important place in the mass motorization of the KKP. However, Tarifa Auto remained a manufacturer external to the autonomy, while Hanul was intended to become a local brand connected with a new stage of industrial development in the northern part of the peninsula.

24 June 2026: discussion in the “Hall of Decisions”

On 24 June 2026, in the “Hall of Decisions,” KKP President Dengi Siltse held a discussion on the region’s economic development. During the meeting, one of the advisers proposed creating the region’s own automobile company, capable of becoming an internal industrial support and reducing dependence on rare imported cars. The idea was supported by the president, after which preparations began for the official formation of the new automaker.

26 June 2026: founding of the company

On 26 June 2026, the automobile company Hanul was officially created. The company’s name is written in Korean as 하늘. From the moment of its foundation, it was regarded not as a niche project, but as a future mass producer of passenger cars for the KKP.

Already on the first working day, starting at nine in the morning, the first employees arrived at the company. They were distributed among early development areas: design, chassis, the general structure of the car, the powertrain, and preparation of the future production base. The rapid start emphasized that Hanul was created not only as a political statement, but also as a practical industrial project.

Start of cooperation with Tarifa Auto and Toyota

At the same time as Hanul was launched, cooperation began with Tarifa Auto, which was already working in the same market and was effectively displacing Hyundai from the everyday automotive space of the KKP. Tarifa Auto’s experience was important for understanding local demand: cheap cars, simple maintenance, repairability, and adaptation for drivers who had only recently entered mass car culture.

The second direction was interaction with Japanese automotive representatives. Materials from the early period emphasized that development of the powertrain system was being carried out jointly with the Japanese side, but the engines themselves were to be produced by Hanul’s own forces. This approach allowed the company to receive engineering support without becoming a simple assembly site for foreign units.

July 2026: first information about the car

By July 2026, Hanul was already working on the design of its first car and further development of its construction. According to early leaks, the first model was expected to receive a hatchback body and a front-wheel-drive layout. This choice matched the company’s task: to create a practical, compact, and comparatively affordable car for the cities and suburbs of the KKP.

Front-wheel drive was considered the most logical solution for the first model: it simplified packaging, reduced production costs, and suited a mass-market city car well. The hatchback, in turn, gave the vehicle versatility — it could be used as family transport, a car for young drivers, and a base model for future modifications.

First project

At the early stage, Hanul’s first car did not have a publicly fixed name. In company materials and leaks, it was described as a front-wheel-drive hatchback at the stage of design, chassis, and general construction development.

  • Body type: hatchback.
  • Layout: front-wheel drive.
  • Purpose: mass-market car for the domestic KKP market.
  • Powertrain system: development with the participation of Japanese automotive representatives.
  • Engine production: Hanul’s own production.
  • Market role: local alternative to rare Hyundai cars and a complement to existing Tarifa Auto models.

Market role

Hanul was created as an automobile brand that was meant to secure the KKP’s own place in passenger-car manufacturing. Unlike Tarifa Auto, which began as a division of a large company, Hanul was formed from the beginning specifically as a regional automaker of the northern part of the Korean Peninsula.

The company’s main task was not immediate international expansion, but the creation of a clear domestic product: a car that could be mass-produced, serviced, and used in KKP cities. At the same time, cooperation with Tarifa Auto and Toyota allowed Hanul not to begin development completely from zero.

Technologies and development

The early development stage was focused on basic automotive areas: chassis, body architecture, design, powertrain, and preparation of future production processes. Special attention was paid to the powertrain system, since in-house engine production was supposed to become an important sign of Hanul’s independence.

Joint work with Japanese specialists was viewed as a way to gain access to an engineering school focused on reliability and service life. However, the company itself emphasized a course toward localization: key units, including engines, were to be produced within the Hanul system.

Features

  • Regional identity — the company was created as the KKP’s own automaker.
  • Late start — Hanul appeared only in 2026, when the regional market already had experience with mass motorization.
  • Cooperation with Tarifa Auto — use of the experience of a company that had long worked with affordable cars for the KKP.
  • Japanese engineering participation — assistance in developing the powertrain system while preserving a course toward in-house production.
  • Focus on a hatchback — the first project was aimed at a practical mass-market body, not an expensive image model.

Criticism and questions

At the early stage, Hanul faced several questions. The main one was the ability of the new company to quickly move from a political decision to real serial production. The complexity of producing its own engines was also discussed separately: for a young brand, this required not only an engine project, but also production discipline, quality control, suppliers, testing, and a service base.

The balance between independence and external assistance also raised questions. On the one hand, cooperation with Tarifa Auto and Japanese specialists reduced the risk of failure for the first model. On the other hand, Hanul had to prove that it was not simply a local brand with foreign technologies, but a full-fledged automobile company of the KKP.
