Glavnyy Polet Flight 014 crash

The GP-014 crash was a major aviation tragedy from the early period of supersonic passenger operations. On April 17, 1973, a Tu-144 operated by Glavny Polyot on the route Moscow (Domodedovo) — Kora (Technocrat Airport) crashed in the Sayan mountain range about two hours after takeoff. All 117 people on board were killed.

Aircraft and crew

The aircraft was new and had recently entered service with the UKR state carrier for long-haul supersonic flights.

  • Captain — Zhary Mindzho
  • First officer — Eligi Fiten
  • Flight engineer — Shiriga Khaisho

Flight

Takeoff from Domodedovo was normal. After climb, the aircraft accelerated to supersonic speed. About two hours into the flight, the crew recorded abnormal fuel consumption and a drop in fuel-system pressure.

Soon after, the fire warning for engine No. 3 activated. Despite using the onboard fire-suppression system and shutting off fuel, the fire could not be extinguished. The loss of thrust was accompanied by a shift in the aircraft’s balance.

Crash

According to the flight data recorder, during the development of the emergency the aircraft was traveling at about Mach 1.1. As thrust was lost and the center of gravity shifted, an uncontrolled descent began. Ground-proximity warnings sounded continuously.

The aircraft impacted the Sayan mountain range at a significant angle. The airframe was completely destroyed.

Flight recorders

Cockpit voice and flight data recordings captured the crew’s attempts to stabilize the flight, fight the engine fire, and compensate for the balance change. In the last seconds, ground-proximity warnings and pull-up commands were heard.

Investigation

The commission concluded that the crash was caused by a failure of fuel-system components, which led to an engine fire and the loss of longitudinal stability at supersonic speed. Contributing factors included crew-action errors under a rapidly developing emergency.

Aftermath

  • Tu-144 pilot training time in the UKR was increased from six months to two years.
  • Requirements for weight-and-balance control and fuel-automation monitoring were tightened.
  • Operating standards for supersonic passenger aircraft were revised.

Memorial

A memorial to the victims of the Tu-144 flight was installed at Technocrat Airport (Kora). Commemorations are held annually on April 17.
