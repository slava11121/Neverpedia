Tarifa Auto

Tarifa Auto is the passenger-car division of Tarifa, established in 2011 to launch affordable civilian cars, primarily for Kivish Korean Peninsula (KKP). The division is known for the “people’s” model Misi-N, a short-run mid-size sedan Style, and the niche project Revint Di3 aimed at late-2010s fan culture.

History

2011: Division creation and the launch of Misi-N

Tarifa Auto was formed in 2011 when the state needed to quickly strengthen public trust in KKP and start mass driver training. A key requirement was a modest, cheap, and “non-intimidating” car for beginners. F-KAP, focused on full-size mainstream cars, could not offer such a minimalist concept, and Tarifa took the task.

The first model was Tarifa Misi-N, an extremely simple city car. The technical philosophy was built around the lowest possible cost of ownership: lightweight solutions were used, and a reworked power unit derived from an early-2000s Tarifa commercial engine (simplified by reducing the number of cylinders). At the same time, the passenger direction received its own symbolism/logo variant to separate the passenger lineup from trucks and special machinery.

2013–2017: Style and the “short era” of passenger expansion

In 2013, Tarifa attempted to enter the mid-size passenger-car segment with Tarifa Style. The model was perceived as an unexpected competitor to F-KAP’s passenger line: public attention in 2013–2014 was fueled by the very fact that a “van and tractor maker” presented a modern-looking sedan. By 2014, the initial buzz naturally faded and the market returned to calm brand selection.

In 2017, Style production ended: a limited number of cars were built, after which the project was closed as a completed experiment that did not become the base for a large model family.

2019–2021: Revint Di3 as a fan project

In 2019, Tarifa launched the niche model Tarifa Revint Di3, conceived as a “Skyline R34 era car” — a reference to the aesthetics and culture of past street racing, especially in demand on the Japanese direction. The project had strong image character: it was discussed in fan communities, one car was used in a movie, but the market remained narrow. In 2021, sales and production ended: for a niche coupe, 2–3 years of series life was seen as a successful outcome.

2022–2025: Pause during the update of Tarifa’s main lineup

After Tarifa’s major 2022 platform refresh (commercial transport, minivans, tractors, and special machinery), Tarifa Auto entered several years of minimal change, focusing on supporting existing models and service infrastructure. Rebadging ideas (putting a Tarifa badge on ready-made foreign cars) were discussed, but rejected as reputationally toxic for a brand valued for honest engineering identity.

2023: End of Misi-N production

In 2023, Misi-N production was permanently stopped. The last car was sold to Li Yong Jun; the dealer center marked the deal symbolically with applause and party poppers as “the end of an era” for the division’s most affordable and mass model.

2026: Tarifa Minic and the “boring year”

In 2026, Tarifa Auto released the Tarifa Minic hatchback, which critics called “absolutely ordinary”: the model was neither a failure nor a hit and was perceived as a “segment filler” without bold solutions. Online discussions appeared suggesting that the division had lost its earlier drive and no longer matched inflated expectations formed during the Misi-N and Style periods.

Models

  • Misi-N (2011–2023) — an ultra-affordable city car for KKP mass motorization.
  • Style (2013–2017) — a mid-size sedan, short-run without continuation.
  • Revint Di3 (2019–2021) — an image project in a late-1990s style aimed at fans.
  • Minic (since 2026) — a standard hatchback perceived as a “neutral” product.

Traits

  • Regional start — the division was created to meet KKP needs rather than as a typical commercial launch.
  • Pragmatic engineering — focus on low ownership cost, simple units, and repairability.
  • Image spikes — Style and Revint Di3 worked for reputation and buzz more than for mass market share.

Criticism

Tarifa Auto was criticized for the lack of long product cycles (fast closure of Style and Di3) and for “losing the spark” in the mid-2020s, when Minic was perceived as faceless. At the same time, the division was often praised for its firm stance against rebadging and for Misi-N’s role in KKP mass motorization.
