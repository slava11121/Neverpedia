Dart model history

Tarifa Dart did not come from the company as a whole, but from a separate Tarifa commercial van line. Its foundation was the FGR1, introduced in 1980 as a working van for heavy everyday use. Even then, the line was built around practicality: one base, several versions for different tasks.

In 1984, the FGR2 was released — a tougher and more refined generation developed after criticism of the FGR1’s pulling power under load. In 1995, Tarifa launched the FGR4.Series with a new body, a new engine, and a revised systems architecture. In 2000, the FGR name was retired and the entire commercial line was renamed Dart.

The next key milestone came in 2005, when Dart received a new look that became one of Tarifa’s most recognizable design works. After that, the model was updated for a long time without a drastic change in appearance, and in 2022 the line went through a full major update that brought Dart to its modern form.
