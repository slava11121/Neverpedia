About the plant

History

AirHokol-66 was founded in 1932 in Kora at 124 Toromskaya Street. Initially, the enterprise developed military aviation equipment, and later became one of the key manufacturers of civil passenger and cargo aircraft.

Formation of the plant

The early stage of AirHokol-66 was connected with the creation of its own engineering and production base. The plant gradually moved from military programs to the development of large-scale civil platforms, which defined its future role in the aviation industry.

Development of civil programs

Over time, AirHokol-66 expanded its aircraft lineup: from light and regional categories to wide-body, medium-haul, and long-haul platforms. This is how the plant established itself as a major civil aviation manufacturer.

Engineering systems

Systems and safety

An important part of AirHokol-66 is not only the aircraft themselves, but also engineering solutions related to stabilization, emergency notification, onboard safety, rapid post-crash assistance, and crew support systems.

“Clock System”

One of the most recognizable engineering features of AirHokol-66, associated with stabilization and the overall technical identity of the company’s passenger platforms.

SDRS

A system that reads data from the aircraft’s onboard sensors and in real time transmits information about specific events and faults to ground dispatch: fire, critical failures, lack of crew activity, and other emergency conditions.

RRC

The Rapid Rescue Compartment is a separate emergency compartment designed for survival after a crash. It contains a supply of water, food, clothing, and basic necessities for the first hours after the accident.

AIHP

An AI pilot-assistance system that complements classic onboard solutions and strengthens crew interaction with the aircraft in newer generations of AirHokol-66 aviation platforms.
